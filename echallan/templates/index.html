Driving License Requirement

Every driver must carry a valid driving license to operate a vehicle legally. Driving without a license can result in fines or imprisonment, depending on the offense. The license ensures that the driver has undergone proper training and knows the rules of the road, contributing to overall traffic safety.

Always Own Valid Car Registration

It is mandatory for all vehicles on the road to have valid registration documents. Driving an unregistered car can lead to fines, vehicle impoundment, or legal consequences. Registration ensures that the vehicle meets safety standards, is properly insured, and that the ownership details are recorded with the government authorities. Fines for driving without valid registration can range from Rs.1000 to Rs.5000.

Wear your seatbelt while Driving a Car

If you are new to driving, get in the habit of securing the seat belt as the first thing you do upon entering your vehicle. Doing so will not only help you avoid traffic violations, but it will also save your life in case of mishaps. If you are caught driving without this seat belt around your waist and chest, traffic cops can fine you up to Rs.1000 for this violation on the spot. So, be a hero and wear your seat belt!

Riding a Two-Wheeler without a Helmet On

One must wear a helmet at all times while riding a two-wheeler. One distinction to notice here is that the law states that all individuals on a two-wheeler must put on helmets and not just the driver. Penalties for non-compliance with this rule come in the form of fines of up to Rs.1000. In serious cases, traffic authorities may decide to suspend your license for a period of up to 3 months as well.

Using a Mobile Phone while Driving

Using a mobile phone while driving without a hands-free device is illegal and a leading cause of distracted driving accidents. It significantly impairs reaction time and focus on the road. The fine for mobile phone use while driving can range from Rs.500 to Rs.2000, depending on the severity of the violation.

Over Speeding

Adhering to speed limits is crucial for road safety. In urban areas, the speed limit typically ranges from 50-60 km/h, while on highways and motorways, it can go up to 120 km/h. Speeding not only increases the risk of accidents but also leads to heavy fines, usually ranging from Rs.1000 to Rs.5000. Staying within the prescribed limits helps prevent collisions and ensures a safer journey.

Jumping the Red Light

If you do not intend to bear penalties of up to Rs.5000 and a one-year prison sentence, ensure you stick to the various traffic signals during a drive, even if you are in a hurry. Remember the old saying, ‘better late than never.’ These are some of the most basic traffic laws for drivers. As a person behind the wheels, you need to follow several others as well. Drive safe and adhere to all laws!
